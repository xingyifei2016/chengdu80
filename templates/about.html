Overview of FREE

FREE is more than an open-sourced financial researcher search engine. Packed with cutting-edge data analytics techniques, such as unsupervised statistical learning, knowledge graph embedding and network analysis, along with many compelling interactive visualizations, FREE can demonstrate a complete and vivid picture of the world of research -- What is a certain researcher most known for? What are the research trends now? How does each research community look like?

We have listed a few key features of FREE below.

Complete

FREE contains information of an astounishingly 26,670 researchers, 1,904,355 publications in 138 different fields of financial research.

Fast

FREE is lightning fast, each search query takes less than 0.001s to respond.

Detailed

FREE provides detailed analytics of each individual researcher and research field. For each researcher, we provide a detailed summarization and analysis of his / her research life: relevant papers, research interest, collaborative network, impact graph, citation statistics, potential relevant researchers, etc. For each field, we provide a detailed summarization and analysis of this field: community networks, key researchers, key publications, etc.

User-Friendly

FREE gives intuitive explanations of all statistical terms, i.e. what is this statistics measuring? Also, many figures in FREE are interactive, so that users may find their own desired piece of information.

Features of FREE

We provide two ways of searching, by researchers or by fields. Our search is lightning fast and each page contains rich information regarding the desired unit.

Search for Researchers

Please use this link to jump to the researcher search page. Alternatively, you may click "Search by Name" on the top menu bar.

You may search your desired researcher based on his / her name, institute affiliation, and / or expertise. For example, if you are interested in Professor John Denero of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science department in University of California, Berkeley, you may input "John Denero" in the Name field, "Berkeley" in Institute field and "Computer Science" in the Expertise field. And the first returned unit is Professor John Denero`s page in FREE engine.

If instead of identifying the information of an exact Professor, you want to explore Professors in certain expertise. FREE also gets you covered! For example, you may simply type "financial engineering" in Expertise to get a list of Professors who work in this field. And if you want to specify Professors in University of California, Berkeley working on financial engineering, you may input "financial engineering" in Expertise and "berkeley" in Institute. Good luck exploring!

You may take a look at a sample page here.

Search for Fields

Please use this link to jump to the field search page. Alternatively, you may click "Search by Field" on the top menu bar.

You may search your interested field based on American Economics Association`s JEL classification code. Here, we only include category and sub-category. For example, if you are interested in quantitative economics and especially single equation methods, you may input "quantitative" in Field and "single equation" in Subfield.
